Words That Sell:
Copywriting Before Coding

Amateurs open a website builder and start dragging random boxes. Professionals start by writing a persuasive argument. Let's use AI to craft the sales copy that will make Aisha's clients beg to book her.

The PAS Framework

If Aisha's website just says, "I am a makeup artist, here are my prices," it will fail. Why? Because people buy based on emotion, not logic. To trigger that emotion, we use the PAS Framework: Problem, Agitation, Solution.

First, we state the Problem (You want to look flawless on your big day). Then, we Agitate it (But you're terrified of the makeup melting off or the MUA arriving late). Finally, we present the Solution (Aisha's premium, sweat-proof, punctual bridal service). When a client reads that, they feel understood. And when they feel understood, price becomes irrelevant.

The Execution

Turning Google Gemini into a High-Ticket Copywriter.

The Master Prompt

"Act as an expert direct-response copywriter. My client Aisha is a premium makeup artist in Abuja specializing in high-end bridal looks. Write the copy for her one-page booking website using the PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution) framework.

Please include:
1. A catchy Hero Headline (H1) and Subheadline.
2. A short section addressing the anxiety of bad makeup on a wedding day (Problem/Agitation).
3. Why Aisha is the ultimate, stress-free choice (Solution).
4. 3 Bullet points of her key benefits.
5. A clear Call to Action button text."
