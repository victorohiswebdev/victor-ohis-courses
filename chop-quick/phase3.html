Selling the Sizzle:
Marketing Assets

Ade has a beautiful logo, but a logo doesn't pay the bills—customers do. It is time to design the launch flyers that will make his audience stop scrolling and start ordering.

Visual Hierarchy

If everything on your flyer is shouting, the customer hears nothing. This is the law of Visual Hierarchy. You have to decide exactly what the viewer sees first, second, and third.

For Ade's food business, the hierarchy is simple: 1. The Food (it must be hyper-realistic and mouth-watering to stop the scroll). 2. The Offer (the tagline or launch text). 3. The Action (a clear "Order Now" button). We are going to use Leonardo.ai to generate a hero image that handles step one perfectly.
