Giving Chop-Quick
An Identity

Ade has his colors and his tagline. Now, he needs a logo. But amateurs make a massive mistake here: they generate a pretty picture instead of a scalable brand mark. Let's fix that.

The "Stamp" Rule

When you are generating a logo with AI, you must command the machine to think in Vectors, not Rasters. A raster image (like a photograph) gets blurry and pixelated when you stretch it.

A great logo must pass the "Stamp Test." If you shrink it down to the size of a tiny postage stamp, or make it completely black and white, it must still be highly recognizable. This is exactly why we instruct the AI to avoid 3D shading, shadows, or complex backgrounds. We want clean, flat, bold geometry.
