The Foundation:
Words Before Pixels

Amateurs open Canva and start guessing colors. Professionals start with data, psychology, and a solid brief. Let's use AI as our Chief Strategy Officer to define who Ade is actually selling to.

The Psychology of Color

Have you ever noticed why almost every major fast-food chain uses red or orange in their branding? It's not a coincidence. Warm colors psychologically stimulate appetite and urgency.

However, Ade wants to run a Premium Cloud Kitchen, not a cheap roadside joint. So, we are going to instruct our AI to balance that appetite-inducing vibrant orange with a deep, sophisticated charcoal. This tells the customer: "Our food is hot and delicious, but our service is high-end and professional."

The Execution

We are taking this strategy straight to Google Gemini.

The Master Prompt

"Act as an expert brand strategist. My client, Ade, is starting a modern cloud kitchen in Lagos called 'Chop-Quick', specializing in premium grilled catfish and fries for delivery only.

Please provide:
1. A brief profile of his ideal target audience.
2. A catchy, 3-to-4 word tagline.
3. A 2-color brand palette (with exact HEX codes) that screams 'modern, fast, appetizing, and premium Nigerian street food'."
