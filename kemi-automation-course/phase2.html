The Pipes

We have our Notion database. Now we need the plumbing. We are going to use Make.com to visually connect Mrs. Kemi's Gmail account to our system, so the robot knows exactly when to wake up and work.

Triggers & Actions

To build an invisible robot, you don't need to learn complicated coding languages like Python. You just need to understand Visual Programming. In Make.com, everything boils down to two concepts: Triggers and Actions.

A Trigger is the starting gun. It is the event that wakes the robot up. An Action is the task the robot performs. For Mrs. Kemi, our Trigger will be "Watch for new emails with the subject 'Investment Lead'". When that happens, the robot springs into action.

The Live Build Workflow

"Open a free account on Make.com and click 'Create a New Scenario'. We are going to drag our first bubble onto the screen. This is our Gmail Trigger."

01. The Blank Canvas

Click the big '+' button on your Make.com canvas to add a new module. Search for "Gmail."

02. Watch Emails

Select the "Watch Emails" trigger. This tells the system to constantly monitor Mrs. Kemi's inbox.

03. Authenticate

Click "Add Connection" to safely log into Gmail. Now our robot has permission to read the incoming mail.
